Davey Gibian's work is useful because it bridges two conversations that are often separated: AI security and
organizational innovation. His core warning is direct: mainstream deployment is outrunning safeguards.

[[cite:davey_gibian_ai_biggest_unaddressed_issue_quote]]

The companion insight from his Janes work is that technology gains only matter when organizations can absorb them. That
means collaboration quality, workflow design, and adoption speed are strategic levers, not culture-side extras.

Taken together, these works suggest a practical operating model: treat AI systems as security-critical from day one,
and treat innovation as a repeatable capability tied to mission outcomes.
