AI’s upside case is real: some forecasts project large aggregate output gains.[[cite:goldman_gdp_7_percent_quote]]

But market pressure can force firms to pursue those gains through compressed staffing and faster restructuring cycles.[[cite:reuters_software_stocks_slump_quote]] When that dynamic becomes widespread, employment and income stability can lag behind productivity narratives.[[cite:reuters_amazon_30000_cuts_quote]]

That is the future-of-work policy and strategy challenge: if efficiency gains outpace household earning power, the demand base that companies depend on can weaken. Teams need guardrails that pair automation strategy with capability transition plans, not cost reduction alone.
